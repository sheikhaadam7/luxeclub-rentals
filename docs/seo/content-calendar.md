**Rule:** before assigning an anchor to a new pitch, glance at the running count. If any bucket is already at or over its target proportion, substitute with the most-under-indexed bucket.

**Sanity rule:** the exact-match bucket is **7 articles for the entire year**. That is less than one per month. Use it sparingly and save it for the highest-authority placements where it will do the most work. Almost every other pitch should default to branded, URL, or generic.

## Monthly review checklist

Run this on the 1st of each month. Takes ~10 minutes.

- [ ] **Check anchor ratios** — pull current running % from the anchor tracker. Are any buckets more than 5 percentage points over target? If yes, next month's pitches must use the under-indexed bucket(s).
- [ ] **Check target URL spread** — is the homepage at or above 25% of total links? If yes, do not link the homepage again until the ratio rebalances. Is any single URL at more than 10% of total? If yes, that URL is over-indexed — diversify.
- [ ] **Check seasonality shift** — are we approaching September 2026? Starting August, begin shifting Apr–Aug's 60/15/25 geographic split toward Sep+'s 55/10/30/5. Starting September, mainstream tourism angles come back online.
- [ ] **Review DR of recent placements** — the last 30 days of published articles, what's the average Domain Rating band? If it's trending down (more mediums, fewer highs), you're spending insider favors on lower-value slots. Reprioritise P1 outlets.
- [ ] **Pick next month's target mix** — update the pitch queue with the next ~8–14 assignments. Diversify across tier 1 outlets, angles, and target URLs. Email your insider with the batch.

## Escalation triggers

Stop posting and reassess immediately if any of these happen:

- **Any Ahrefs / Semrush / Google Search Console alert about unusual link growth or unnatural backlink profile.** This is the #1 sign you're over-posting or too concentrated on a single anchor/target. Pause for 14 days and audit.
- **A pattern of three consecutive pitch rejections from Tier 1 outlets with the same reason ("we don't cover this topic" / "outside our editorial scope").** Means the angle no longer resonates — rotate to different pitches.
- **A manual penalty notice from Google** (very rare but the nuclear scenario). Stop all placements immediately, disavow any suspect links via Search Console, and wait 3–6 months before resuming.
- **An editor at a Tier 1 outlet explicitly asks about compensation or editorial-standards disclosure.** Treat this seriously — it suggests the outlet has internal policies that require the link be marked as sponsored/nofollow, which destroys its SEO value. Either accept the nofollow or decline the placement; don't lie to editors.
