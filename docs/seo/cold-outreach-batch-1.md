## How to find the right editor email

Before sending these, you need the actual editor's name and email. Here's how to find them for each outlet:

1. **Browse their motoring/lifestyle section** — click on 3-4 recent articles about cars or Dubai lifestyle. Note the byline (author name).
2. **LinkedIn search** — search "[author name] [publication name]". Their profile will show their title (e.g. "Motors Editor at Esquire Middle East").
3. **Email pattern** — most UAE publications use firstname.lastname@domain.com or firstname@domain.com. You can verify using Hunter.io (free tier) or simply check the publication's masthead/about page.
4. **If you can't find a specific editor** — use the general editorial email (editorial@whatson.ae, editorial@esquireme.com, etc.) with "FAO: Motors/Lifestyle Editor" in the subject.

**Always pitch a person, not a desk.** "Hi Sarah" converts 5x better than "Dear Editorial Team".

## Sending order and timing

1. **Send Esquire ME first** (Monday morning UAE time, 9-10am) — most likely to respond quickly
2. **Send What's On second** (same day, afternoon) — highest acceptance probability
3. **Send Gulf News third** (Tuesday morning) — highest value if it lands, lowest probability

**Do NOT send all three on the same day at the same time.** If two outlets accept, you need to offer exclusivity on the data — "we haven't published this elsewhere yet" is a selling point. Stagger by 24-48 hours so you can offer the first responder a short exclusivity window.

**If an editor responds asking for the full draft:** send the article from `linkbait-article.md` immediately (remove the "Instructions for placing this piece" section at the bottom — that's internal). Respond within 2 hours. Speed is everything with editors.

**If no response after 5 business days:** send ONE follow-up. "Hi [name], just bumping this in case it got buried — happy to send the full draft if the angle works for your section." No more than one follow-up. If they don't reply to the follow-up, move on.

**After a placement publishes:** log it in `content-calendar.md`, update the anchor and target URL trackers, then wait 7 days before pitching the same article to a different outlet. Do NOT pitch the same piece to a competing publication simultaneously — if an editor discovers you pitched the same "exclusive data" to their rival, you're burned at both outlets permanently.
